# Solstein — Brand Identity

This document defines the Solstein brand. Use it to keep the website, the application UI, and any other surface visually consistent. It is written to be fed directly to Claude Code as a styling reference.

## About the Parent Brand (Nile)

Solstein is being built to eventually sit within the offering of Nile — also known as Nile HQ or the Nile Group. The following is a summary of Nile's brand, gathered from their public material, so that Solstein can be designed as a credible member of that family. Note: this is inferred from Nile's website copy, newsletter, B Corp impact reports, and social presence. It has not been taken from an official Nile brand guidelines document, so the exact colour values and typefaces below should be confirmed and substituted once Nile's real brand assets are available.

Nile is a strategic design consultancy founded in 2006 by Sarah Ronald, headquartered in Edinburgh's New Town. It is a certified B Corp and works on AI transformation for regulated industries — banking, insurance, healthcare. Its positioning line is "smarter decisions using insight, not opinion." Its vision is "a world where nobody is left behind by the systems that shape our lives."

The most important discovery for Solstein: Nile's brand world is already built on navigation. Their newsletter is called *The Navigator*. They describe their team as people who "clear the path to progress." Their language is full of steering, helm, horizon-scanning, signals, cutting through complexity, and finding bearing. This means Solstein — named for a Viking navigation instrument — is not merely compatible with Nile; it is native to their world. The sunstone is, quite literally, the instrument the Navigator uses. This connection should be treated as the central bridge between the two brands.

Nile's character is warm, intelligent, human, and quietly confident — plain-spoken rather than corporate, curious and a little playful, never boastful. Accessibility and inclusion are core values (Nile acquired the accessibility firm Dig Inclusion, and publishes screen-reader-friendly versions of everything). Any Solstein surface must therefore meet accessibility standards as a matter of brand principle, not afterthought.

## What Solstein Is

Solstein is a cinematic operational and data architecture intelligence tool. It takes a structured graph of how an organisation actually works — systems, data, workflows, decisions, ownership, risk — and turns it into a living, explorable visual map. The product reveals hidden complexity that conventional diagrams cannot.

The brand must do two things at once: feel visually impressive and technically serious. It should never feel like a toy, a generic SaaS template, or a superficial visual demo.

## The Name

Solstein refers to the Viking sunstone — a calcite crystal (Iceland spar) used to navigate when cloud cover hid the sun. Light passing through it revealed direction that was otherwise invisible. This is the core brand metaphor: finding clarity and bearing when the real picture is obscured. The navigation heritage should be felt subtly, never illustrated literally with longships, runes, or Norse pastiche.

## Brand Personality

Five words: clear, precise, cinematic, credible, calm. Solstein is the instrument in the room that everyone trusts. It is confident without being loud, technical without being cold, and beautiful without being decorative. It sits comfortably alongside a strategic design consultancy's existing work — measured, intelligent, and quietly distinctive.

## Relationship to the Parent Brand

Solstein should feel like a credible member of the Nile family without copying it directly.

The strongest connection is conceptual, not visual. Nile's whole brand is about navigation and wayfinding — *The Navigator*, clearing the path, steering, scanning the horizon. Solstein extends that exact metaphor into a specialist tool: the sunstone is the instrument that makes navigation possible when the way is obscured. Lean on this. In copy and positioning, Solstein helps you "find your bearing" in operational complexity, "see through the fog," "take a true reading" of how a system works. This shared language is what makes Solstein unmistakably part of Nile's world.

The visual connection is established through shared restraint — generous space, confident typography, disciplined colour, calm motion, and a serious, intelligent tone. It is not established by matching Nile's palette or layouts exactly.

Solstein has its own distinct identity within that family: darker, more cinematic, more instrument-like than the parent consultancy. Nile is the practice; Solstein is the precision instrument the practice carries into the room. If Nile's own surfaces are lighter and more editorial, Solstein is allowed to be the dark, focused, technical counterpart — the difference between a studio and an observation deck.

When Nile's official brand assets become available, align Solstein's neutral foundations and typography more closely with them, but keep Solstein's dark, instrument-like character intact.

## Logo

The logo is the Crystal mark plus the Solstein wordmark.

The mark is an octagonal geometric crystal — the Iceland spar form — drawn in fine line work. It contains an inner refraction outline at lower opacity, a central focal point representing the located sun, and a faint cross axis. It should always be drawn with thin, precise strokes. It must never be filled solid, gradient-filled, or given heavy weight.

The wordmark is SOLSTEIN, always uppercase, set in Barlow Condensed Medium with wide letter-spacing (0.18em). The mark sits to the left of the wordmark, vertically centred, separated by clear space roughly equal to the mark's width.

Do not add a tagline, "by Nile", or any descriptor inside the logo lockup. The logo stands alone.

Clear space around the logo equals the height of the crystal mark on all sides. Minimum display width for the full lockup is 120px; below that, use the crystal mark alone.

## Colour Palette

Solstein runs on a dark foundation with a single warm accent. The discipline is the point — one accent, used sparingly, carries all the meaning.

**Core**
- Deep Navigation `#0F1115` — primary background
- Crystal Black `#0A0C0F` — deepest panels, canvas ground
- Surface `#16191F` — raised panels, cards, inspector background

**Accent**
- Sunstone Amber `#E8C97A` — the single brand accent. Used for the logo, key highlights, active states, focal points. Never used as a large fill — it works as line, glow, and small mark only.

**Neutrals**
- Bone `#F0EDE8` — primary text on dark
- Mist `rgba(240,237,232,0.55)` — secondary text
- Faint `rgba(240,237,232,0.3)` — tertiary text, captions
- Hairline `rgba(255,255,255,0.07)` — borders and dividers

**Light mode (documents, exports, light website sections)**
- Paper `#F4F2EE` — light background
- Ink `#1A1C1F` — text and marks on light

**Semantic colours** (used only inside the application for overlays and graph states — keep them muted, never neon)
- PII / sensitive: Violet `#9B8FC4`
- Manual / friction: Amber `#D8A24A`
- Risk: Muted Red `#C66B5E`
- Systems / integration: Steel Blue `#6B8CB0`
- Validated / healthy: Sage `#7FA67F`
- Unknown / low confidence: Grey `#6E7178`

## Typography

Two typefaces, both from the Barlow family, for a consistent and slightly technical voice.

**Barlow Condensed** — display and structural. Used for the wordmark, headings, labels, button text, and any uppercase element. Headings and labels are uppercase with wide tracking (0.12em–0.25em). Weights: 400, 500, 600.

**Barlow** — body and reading text. Used for paragraphs, descriptions, longer-form content. Set at comfortable line height (1.6–1.8). Weights: 300, 400, 500. Body copy is sentence case, never uppercase.

**Hierarchy**
- Page title: Barlow Condensed Medium, uppercase, 0.12em tracking
- Section label / eyebrow: Barlow Condensed Medium, uppercase, 0.2em–0.25em tracking, ~11px, in Sunstone Amber at reduced opacity
- Body: Barlow Light or Regular, sentence case, ~14–16px
- Caption / meta: Barlow Light, ~10–12px, in Faint neutral

Never use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or system fonts anywhere in the brand.

## Layout and Space

Generous negative space is mandatory. The brand reads as calm and confident because it is not crowded. Use a clear grid, consistent gutters, and let panels breathe.

Borders are hairline-thin. Corners are nearly square — use a 2px border radius, not rounded, not pill-shaped. The aesthetic is precise and instrument-like, not soft.

Dividers are single hairline rules at very low opacity. Use them to separate sections rather than boxes-within-boxes.

## Motion

Motion is smooth, deliberate, and meaningful — never decorative. Transitions sit around 0.3s with an ease curve. On page load, use a single well-orchestrated staggered reveal rather than many scattered micro-animations. The crystal mark may carry a very subtle light or refraction animation, but it should be slow and quiet. If everything moves, nothing matters.

## Imagery and Iconography

Icons are fine line-art only, matching the weight of the crystal mark. No filled icons, no duotone, no rounded cartoon style.

Avoid literal Viking imagery entirely — no runes, longships, axes, or horned helmets. The navigation heritage lives in the language and the crystal, not in illustration.

Where imagery is used, favour dark, atmospheric, abstract textures — depth, subtle grain, faint grid structure — over photography. Any photography should be restrained and desaturated, never stocky.

## Tone of Voice

Write plainly and with authority. Short, declarative sentences. Confident but never hyped. The product reveals difficult truths about how organisations work, so the voice is honest and precise rather than reassuring or salesy.

Say "see how it actually works", not "unlock powerful insights". Avoid exclamation marks, buzzwords, and growth-hacking language. The reader is an intelligent professional — a consultant, architect, or senior stakeholder.

Echo Nile's voice deliberately. Nile's positioning is "insight, not opinion" — Solstein's voice should carry the same evidence-led, grounded confidence. Borrow Nile's navigation vocabulary where it fits naturally: bearing, reading, signal, horizon, steer, the path. Match Nile's warmth — intelligent and human, never cold or corporate, occasionally lightly playful, never boastful. The two brands should sound like they come from the same people.

## Accessibility

Accessibility is a brand principle, inherited directly from Nile, which is a B Corp and owns an accessibility practice. It is not optional polish.

All text must meet WCAG AA contrast against its background. On the dark foundation, Bone text on Deep Navigation passes comfortably; secondary and tertiary greys must be checked and kept above the AA threshold for their text size — do not let "Faint" neutral fall below it for any text the user needs to read. Sunstone Amber on the dark ground must be verified for any text use; if it fails at small sizes, use it for marks, lines, and large elements only, and use Bone for small text.

Never rely on colour alone to carry meaning. Overlay states, risk indicators, and graph semantics must also be distinguished by shape, label, icon, or pattern, so the product remains legible to colour-blind users. Interactive elements need visible focus states. Motion should respect the `prefers-reduced-motion` setting and fall back to instant transitions when it is set.

## Quick Reference for Implementation

```
--sol-bg:         #0F1115;
--sol-ground:     #0A0C0F;
--sol-surface:    #16191F;
--sol-accent:     #E8C97A;
--sol-text:       #F0EDE8;
--sol-text-2:     rgba(240,237,232,0.55);
--sol-text-3:     rgba(240,237,232,0.3);
--sol-hairline:   rgba(255,255,255,0.07);
--sol-paper:      #F4F2EE;
--sol-ink:        #1A1C1F;

--font-display: 'Barlow Condensed', sans-serif;  /* headings, labels, wordmark */
--font-body:    'Barlow', sans-serif;            /* body copy */

--radius: 2px;
--transition: 0.3s ease;
```

Core rules: dark foundation, one amber accent used sparingly, two Barlow weights, hairline borders, 2px radius, generous space, calm motion, no Viking clichés, no generic SaaS aesthetics, WCAG AA contrast throughout, never colour alone for meaning.
